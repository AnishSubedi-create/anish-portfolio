About

Anish Subedi

I work at the boundary where wet-lab biology meets computational science and biophysics. As a Research Technician at the UNM Health Sciences Center, my days move between bench experiments — running qPCR, executing protein biochemistry workflows for mass spectrometry, and conducting BSL-2 viral infection assays — and building automated analysis pipelines in Python and R for adenoviral protein interactomes, RNA-Seq datasets, and biomolecular condensate prediction. I have integrated pyDNA-EPBD, a DNA breathing dynamics tool developed at Los Alamos National Laboratory’s Theoretical Division, into my ongoing virology research, and I am actively exploring the biophysics of liquid-liquid phase separation in viral replication centers.

Science, for me, has never been separate from storytelling. I am a lifelong writer and poet, and I believe that how we communicate ideas — with precision, with care, with narrative — is as important as the ideas themselves. That conviction has driven me toward digital media as a tool for social change. In collaboration with Scott MacLennan, CEO of The Mountain Fund, I have produced Nepali-language awareness videos addressing exploitative foreign labor schemes that have reached over 1.3 million viewers combined.

I am a Summa Cum Laude graduate of the University of New Mexico (GPA 4.14), a co-author on a manuscript under review at iScience (Cell Press), and an active contributor to NIH R21 and R01 grant proposals. My doctoral goal is biophysics.

Research & Tools

Review Paper · In Preparation

HMGB1: Neuroinflammation, Viral Disease & Therapeutic Perspectives

A systematic review examining HMGB1’s multifaceted roles in neuroinflammatory cascades, viral pathogenesis, and immune evasion — including computational LLPS/condensate analysis for HMGB1 interactions with RAGE, TLR9, and TLR4 and its therapeutic potential across adenoviral and other disease contexts.

In Preparation
Computational Tool · In Development

FuzDrop LLPS Analyzer & Condensate Prediction Platform

A Python-based platform chaining NCBI sequence retrieval with FuzDrop scoring to classify liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) competency across adenoviral replication center proteins, HMGB-1, and pathogen receptors — motivated by observed puncta morphology in BSL-2 adenoviral infection assays.

GitHub →
Deployed Tool · Active Lab Use

Viral Infection Plausibility Engine (VIPE)

A publicly deployed Python tool (GitHub Pages) aggregating literature-reported virus-cell receptor interactions to enable rapid plausibility assessment of novel infection hypotheses, eliminating ~10 hours of manual literature search per experiment.

GitHub → Live Tool →
Pipeline · Active Lab Use

AP-MS / RNA-Seq Automated Analysis Pipelines (R)

Automated R pipelines for AP-MS/Co-IP proteomics (intensity-weighted differential enrichment, heatmaps, Venn diagrams) and RNA-Seq (alignment, QC, differential expression) — both adopted as standard tools in the Chodosh & Rajaiya Lab, processing 5+ datasets across 3 active projects.

GitHub →
Computational Tool · In Development

Viral Spillover Risk Predictor (ESM-2)

An AI-powered Python tool harnessing the Meta ESM-2 deep learning protein language model to analyze cross-species protein homology and generate quantitative risk assessments for viral spillover events from animal reservoirs to human hosts.
