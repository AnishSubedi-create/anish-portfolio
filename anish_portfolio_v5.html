About

Anish Subedi

I work at the boundary where wet-lab biology meets computational science and biophysics. As a Research Technician at the UNM Health Sciences Center, my days move between bench experiments, running qPCR, executing protein biochemistry workflows for mass spectrometry, and conducting BSL-2 viral infection assays, and building automated analysis pipelines in Python and R for adenoviral protein interactomes, RNA-Seq datasets, and biomolecular condensate prediction. I have integrated pyDNA-EPBD, a DNA breathing dynamics tool developed at the Theoretical Division of Los Alamos National Laboratory, into my ongoing virology research, and I am actively exploring the biophysics of liquid-liquid phase separation in viral replication centers.

Science, for me, has never been separate from storytelling. I am a lifelong writer and poet, and I believe that how we communicate ideas, with precision, with care, with narrative, is as important as the ideas themselves. That conviction has driven me toward digital media as a tool for social change. In collaboration with Scott MacLennan, CEO of The Mountain Fund, I have produced Nepali-language awareness videos addressing exploitative foreign labor schemes that have reached over 1.3 million viewers combined.

I am a Summa Cum Laude graduate of the University of New Mexico (GPA 4.14), a co-author on a manuscript under review at iScience (Cell Press), and an active contributor to NIH R21 and R01 grant proposals. My doctoral goal is biophysics.

Research & Tools

Review Paper · In Preparation

HMGB1: Neuroinflammation, Viral Disease & Therapeutic Perspectives

A systematic review examining HMGB1's multifaceted roles in neuroinflammatory cascades, viral pathogenesis, and immune evasion, including computational LLPS and condensate analysis for HMGB1 interactions with RAGE, TLR9, and TLR4, with focus on therapeutic potential across adenoviral and other disease contexts.

In Preparation
Deployed Tool · Active Lab Use

Viral Infection Plausibility Engine (VIPE)

A publicly deployed Python tool aggregating literature-reported virus-cell receptor interactions to enable rapid plausibility assessment of novel infection hypotheses, eliminating approximately 10 hours of manual literature search per experiment.

GitHub Live Tool
Deployed Tool · 3D Visualization

CpG DNA 3D Viewer & Receptor Docking Simulations

An interactive 3D visualization tool for CpG DNA structures, receptor conformations, and molecular docking simulations, providing spatial insight into DNA-receptor interactions relevant to innate immune signaling and viral pathogen recognition.

GitHub Live Viewer
Computational Tool · In Development

FuzDrop LLPS Analyzer & Condensate Prediction Platform

A Python-based platform chaining NCBI sequence retrieval with FuzDrop scoring to classify liquid-liquid phase separation competency across adenoviral replication center proteins, HMGB-1, and pathogen receptors, motivated by observed phase separation-like puncta behavior in BSL-2 adenoviral infection assays.

GitHub
Pipeline · Active Lab Use

AP-MS / RNA-Seq Automated Analysis Pipelines (R)

Automated R pipelines for AP-MS/Co-IP proteomics with intensity-weighted differential enrichment, heatmaps, and Venn diagrams, and for RNA-Seq alignment, QC, and differential expression. Both are adopted as standard tools in the Chodosh and Rajaiya Lab, processing five or more datasets across three active projects.

GitHub
Computational Tool · In Development

Viral Spillover Risk Predictor (ESM-2)

An AI-powered Python tool harnessing the Meta ESM-2 deep learning protein language model to analyze cross-species protein homology and generate quantitative risk assessments for viral spillover events from animal reservoirs to human hosts.
